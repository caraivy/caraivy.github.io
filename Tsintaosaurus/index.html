Tsintaosaurus

The unicorn dinosaur that was too good to be true

Unicorn No More

It’s been raining on my parade ever since I heard the news. Paleontologists no longer believe that Tsintaosaurus had a forward facing horn on it's forehead like a unicorn, but rather a crest that pointed backwards.

Heartbreaking news to those of us who truly loved the idea of a real live Unicorn Dinosaur, but ergonomically you have to agree it makes sense. Read it and weep, kids.

No unicorn horn, but fun facts:

  • Tsintaosaurus ate plants
  • lived in the late Cretaceous period
  • is a genus of hadrosaurid dinosaur from China
  • was about 27 ft long and weighed 2.5 tons
  • had a duckbill snout
  • walked on all fours, but could rear up on its hind legs
  • probably lived in herds
